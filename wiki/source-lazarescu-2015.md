**Open-access review (fetched this run; JAHA, CC-BY-NC-ND; full PDF galley retrieved).** Lazarescu reviews [[scholar-guy-halsall|Guy Halsall]]'s Worlds of Arthur: Facts and Fictions of the Dark Ages (Oxford, 2014).

**Core report.** The reviewer states the book's first three parts serve as a guide to why we do not possess any consistent information related to King Arthur and to why it is almost impossible to know whether this historical figure did indeed exist or not, while providing a tool-kit for evaluating the claims made in pseudo-histories. He relays Halsall's methodological line that medieval writers saw no sharp distinction between history and legend, so the reader should ask the right questions of the sources rather than expect right answers, setting aside their ideological framing.

**Bearing on the claim.** Secondary/echo-level (a review, not new evidence), but it accurately documents the skeptical mainstream: the surviving sources cannot establish a historical Arthur. Supports [[claim-arthur-legendary-composite]] and disputes [[claim-king-arthur-historical]].
