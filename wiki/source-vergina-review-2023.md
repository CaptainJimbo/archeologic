**Secondary / review article, 2023.** Verified via the ScienceDirect record;
listed here as a *review* (echo), not primary evidence — an important distinction
this project makes explicit.

A comprehensive survey of the Vergina identification debate, weighing the
radiocarbon, osteological, and archaeological arguments across all three Great
Tumulus tombs. It **cites and summarises** the primary forensic work rather than
generating new skeletal evidence, so in the graph it points *up* at the claims
and at [[site-great-tumulus]] as a synthesis node.

Included deliberately as a citogenesis check: a reader who found only this review
might treat "the evidence" as one coherent body, when it is in fact a contested
pile of [[source-bartsiokas-2000]], [[source-musgrave-2010]], and
[[source-bartsiokas-2015]] pulling in different directions.
