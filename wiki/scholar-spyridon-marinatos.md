Greek archaeologist (1901-1974), excavator of [[site-akrotiri-thera]] from 1967. Originator of the volcanic-destruction hypothesis: his 1939 *Antiquity* paper ('The volcanic destruction of Minoan Crete', Antiquity 13: 425-439) proposed that the Thera eruption devastated Minoan Crete — the root claim [[claim-thera-destroyed-minoans]] and the source of nearly every later popular echo.

Included as the protagonist node for this debate — the counterpart to [[scholar-sturt-manning]] in the chronology dispute. Note the epistemic point the graph should surface: Marinatos advanced the theory in 1939, DECADES before Akrotiri was dug and long before the LM IA/LM IB stratigraphy and radiocarbon work that later undercut it. No `source` node is created for the 1939 paper here because it was not fetched this run; this scholar node records the attribution without standing in for the document.
