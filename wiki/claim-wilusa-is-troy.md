The Hittite toponyms **Wilusa** (and Taru(w)isa) are widely equated with Greek **(W)ilios / Troia**, which would place Homer's Troy inside the documented Hittite world — e.g. the Alaksandu treaty, with Alaksandu of Wilusa compared to Alexandros/Paris. Mainstream Anatolian scholarship (Hawkins, Latacz) accepts the equation; it is a main plank of the historicity case.

But [[source-schurr-2019]] argues it rests on little more than name-associations, none individually cogent, and that no western kingdom named in Hittite sources has yet been confirmed by excavation. Contested.
