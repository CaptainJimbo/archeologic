English architect and self-taught linguist who deciphered [[claim-linear-b-greek|Linear B]] in 1952, showing it recorded Greek. An amateur in the field, he approached the script structurally, extending Alice Kober's work into a grid of consonant-vowel sign relations. Working with [[scholar-john-chadwick]] he published the case in 1953 ([[source-ventris-chadwick-1953]]).

He first suspected the language was Etruscan and only concluded it was Greek when his grid values produced recognisable Greek words. Died in a car crash in 1956 at 34, shortly before the joint book Documents in Mycenaean Greek appeared.
