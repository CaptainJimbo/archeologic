**The claim.** Groups collectively labelled the Sea Peoples are a genuine historical phenomenon documented in contemporary Egyptian sources, most prominently the Year 8 reliefs and inscriptions of Ramesses III (c. 1177 BCE) at his mortuary temple of Medinet Habu, which depict land and sea battles against named groups (Peleset, Tjeker, Shekelesh, Denyen, Weshesh and others). They also appear in earlier records of Merneptah and in correspondence from [[site-ugarit]].

**Why this is separated from the causal claim.** This node exists to keep two things apart that popular accounts fuse: (1) that the Sea Peoples were real and militarily active — well supported — versus (2) that they *caused* the Late Bronze Age collapse — contested and leaning refuted (see [[claim-sea-peoples-collapse]]). The Egyptian evidence is royal commemorative propaganda: it establishes the encounters and the Egyptian victory claim, but it does not establish a unified confederation responsible for the region-wide systems failure captured by [[claim-lbac-multicausal]].

**Status.** Supported at 0.85. Note: this attestation is drawn from the well-known Medinet Habu corpus and standard Egyptological literature; no primary Egyptological edition was fetched in this run, so the confidence reflects general scholarly consensus rather than a source node verified here. Eric Cline ([[scholar-eric-cline]]) treats the Medinet Habu evidence at length while cautioning against reading it as the cause of collapse.
