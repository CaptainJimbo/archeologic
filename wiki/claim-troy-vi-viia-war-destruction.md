Troy VI / VIIa (~1300-1180 BC) ended in destruction, and some read the evidence — burning, hastily buried dead, sling bullets — as a violent sack that could underlie the epic. [[source-hansen-1997]] ties a Hittite tablet (KUB XXIII.13) recording warfare c. 1240 BC to the fall of Mycenaean Troy VI.

However, Bronze-Age destruction layers can equally reflect earthquake or internal collapse, and the phasing/dating is debated; [[scholar-frank-kolb]] is skeptical of reading a Homeric war into the stratigraphy. Contested.
