**The popular claim.** The Bronze Age Plinian eruption of Thera (VEI 7) so devastated Minoan Crete — through ashfall, earthquakes and tsunamis — that it destroyed Minoan civilization outright. This is the version in textbooks, documentaries and the Atlantis literature.

**Origin (primary vs echo).** The thesis traces to a single root: Spyridon Marinatos' 1939 *Antiquity* paper (see [[scholar-spyridon-marinatos]]), which proposed volcanic destruction of Minoan Crete decades before Akrotiri was even excavated. Most later popular repetitions are ECHOES of Marinatos, not independent evidence — a textbook citogenesis funnel.

**Why specialists reject it as stated.** The eruption buried [[site-akrotiri-thera]] during the **Late Minoan IA** ceramic phase. But the near-simultaneous destructions of the Cretan palaces and towns ([[site-minoan-crete]]) date archaeologically to **Late Minoan IB** — roughly 30-50 years, and by the radiocarbon [[claim-thera-high-chronology|~1600 BCE eruption date]] as much as ~100-150 years, LATER. This chronological separation is the crux ([[claim-thera-lmib-gap]]). After the eruption the Minoans demonstrably RECOVERED: LM IB is often called the zenith of Minoan material culture, with sites rebuilt (Palaikastro, Zakros, Phaistos). A civilization that flourishes for a generation or more after the alleged killing blow was not destroyed by it.

**What the physical evidence actually shows.** The eruption did reach Crete — verified tsunami deposits at Palaikastro ([[source-bruins-2009]]) and Malia ([[source-lespez-2021]]) — but the run-up was modest (max ~8 m asl, ~400 m inland at Malia) and the impact coastal and local, not island-wide annihilation. The founding stratigraphic exchange ([[source-pichler-schiering-1977]]) further showed there was only ONE eruption, so no second volcanic event coincides with the later LM IB destructions.

**Verdict.** Refuted as literally stated. Held at 0.2 (a popular oversimplification, not a live scholarly position). The defensible residue — that the eruption may have contributed INDIRECTLY to a slower decline — is tracked separately at [[claim-thera-indirect-contribution]].
