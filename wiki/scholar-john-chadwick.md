Cambridge philologist and Greek-dialect specialist who joined [[scholar-michael-ventris]] in 1952 to test and consolidate the [[claim-linear-b-greek|Linear B]] decipherment, supplying the historical-linguistic expertise Ventris lacked. Co-author of the 1953 JHS paper ([[source-ventris-chadwick-1953]]) and of Documents in Mycenaean Greek (1956; 2nd ed. 1973).

After Ventris's death Chadwick became the leading exponent and defender of Mycenaean studies, answering critics such as [[source-beattie-1956|Beattie]] and writing the standard popular account, The Decipherment of Linear B.
