**The sub-claim.** The Ptolemaic pedigree is well documented on its Macedonian Greek side, but two links in Cleopatra VII's immediate ancestry are not securely attested: the identity of her mother, and that of the mother (paternal grandmother) of her father Ptolemy XII, who was himself an illegitimate son of Ptolemy IX. Because those women are unnamed in the sources, historians cannot fully exclude that a minor fraction of Cleopatra's ancestry was non-Greek, potentially Egyptian, Syrian/Greek-Egyptian, or otherwise local. This is why the strong form wholly/purely Greek is less certain than the predominantly-Greek form of [[claim-cleopatra-greek-ancestry]].

**Why it stays contested, not resolved either way.** There is no positive evidence identifying these women as Egyptian or African; equally there is no proof they were purely Macedonian Greek. The honest verdict is uncertainty over a small unknown fraction, not a substantiated case for major non-Greek ancestry.

**Against the strong non-Greek reading.** The most-cited physical evidence for substantial African/Egyptian ancestry in the family was the skeleton in the Octagon at Ephesos, long claimed to be Cleopatra's half-sister Arsinoe IV and used to argue for an African-descended mother. [[source-weber-2025-octagon|Weber et al. 2025]] show, from genetics, morphology and dating, that those remains are in fact an 11-14 year old boy with genetic ancestry from the Italian peninsula or Sardinia, and cannot be Arsinoe IV at all. That removes the main osteological prop for the substantial-African-ancestry claim, without proving the maternal lines were Greek. Held at 0.5 to mark genuine, unresolved uncertainty. See [[scholar-duane-roller]] for the standard historians treatment of the unknown mother.
