British archaeologist (born 1937), Professor of Ancient History and Classical Archaeology at the University of Bristol and a major authority on Minoan Crete. Director of excavations at the Knossos Stratigraphical Museum site.

In 1979 to 1980 his team recovered a deposit of cut-marked children bones in a Late Minoan IB house at Knossos ([[site-minoan-crete]]), which he interpreted as evidence of child sacrifice and possible ritual cannibalism ([[claim-knossos-child-sacrifice]]). The full osteological record was published with S. M. Wall and the anatomist J. H. Musgrave ([[source-wall-musgrave-warren-1986]]). The physical data are widely accepted; Warren sacrificial and cannibalistic reading of them is contested.
