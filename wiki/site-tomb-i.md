The earlier, cist-type tomb under the [[site-great-tumulus]], looted in antiquity
but famous for its Rape of Persephone wall painting. It contained the disturbed
remains of a man, a woman, and a newborn.

Long treated as a secondary tomb, it moved to centre stage with
[[source-bartsiokas-2015]], which argues its male skeleton — with a fused,
pierced knee ([[claim-tomb-i-knee-lameness]]) — is the true Philip II
([[claim-tomb-i-philip-ii]]). Whether that reassignment holds is unresolved.
