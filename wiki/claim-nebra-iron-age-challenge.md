Sub-claim / minority position. In 2020 Rupert Gebhard and Ruediger Krause ([[scholar-gebhard-krause]]) challenged the standard account of the Nebra Sky Disc, arguing that: (1) the disc should be typologically dated to the Iron Age rather than ~1600 BCE; and (2) it may not have been found together with the bronze hoard, and possibly not even on the [[site-nebra-mittelberg]] near Nebra — i.e. the looter-derived find context that anchors the Early Bronze Age date is unreliable.

STATUS: contested and largely, but not universally, rejected. The challenge was published in a peer-reviewed venue by established prehistorians, which makes it a genuine scholarly dispute rather than pseudoarchaeology; the underlying epistemic worry (a looted object with reconstructed context) is legitimate. However it was rapidly and forcefully rebutted by [[scholar-ernst-pernicka]] and a large interdisciplinary team in [[source-pernicka-2020]], who argue it relies on selective use of early/unpublished material, ignores much subsequent work, and distorts court-trial and laboratory evidence (sediment/corrosion attachments, gold chemistry, lead isotopes) that ties the disc to the hoard and the site. The Early Bronze Age dating [[claim-nebra-disc-bronze-age]] remains the consensus.

CONFIDENCE 0.3: this reflects low credence that the Iron-Age / not-from-Nebra hypothesis is correct, while keeping the node open because the challenge is published, not formally withdrawn, and the debate is live.
