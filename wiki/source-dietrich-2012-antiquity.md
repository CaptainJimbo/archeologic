Excavation-team synthesis. Fetched this run as HTML from Cambridge Core via the DOI; the abstract, keyword set and reference list were captured.

The abstract states the case directly: Gobekli Tepe is pushing back the origins of monumentality beyond the emergence of agriculture, and that at the dawn of the Neolithic, hunter-gatherers congregating at [[site-gobekli-tepe]] created social and ideological cohesion through the carving of decorated pillars, dancing, feasting and, almost certainly, the drinking of beer made from fermented wild crops. The keywords list Epipalaeolithic and Pre-Pottery Neolithic A and B, monumentality, congregation, feasting, and alcohol.

This is strong, near-direct support for [[claim-gobekli-tepe-pre-agricultural]]: the builders are characterised as hunter-gatherers and the crops as wild rather than domesticated, placing the monuments before established farming. Authored by the excavation team including [[scholar-oliver-dietrich]] and [[scholar-klaus-schmidt]]. Note the wild-crop detail also grounds the nuance that these communities were already intensively processing wild cereals. Primary interpretive report by the excavators, not a textbook echo.
