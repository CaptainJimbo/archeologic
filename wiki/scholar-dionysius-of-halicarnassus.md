Greek historian and rhetorician active in Rome, 1st century BCE. In Roman Antiquities (Book 1) he rejected Herodotus's Lydian-migration story and argued the Etruscans were autochthonous — indigenous to Italy, an ancient people resembling no other in language or customs. His autochthonous position anticipates the modern genetic finding that Etruscans were largely a LOCAL population ([[source-posth-2021]], [[source-ghirotto-2013]]), and stands opposite the Eastern-origin claim [[claim-etruscan-anatolian-origin]]. Ancient literary testimony, weighed as a competing claim rather than as evidence.
