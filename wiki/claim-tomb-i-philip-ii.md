A third position, advanced by [[scholar-antonis-bartsiokas]] in
[[source-bartsiokas-2015]]: Philip II is not in Tomb II at all, but in the
adjacent, earlier, looted [[site-tomb-i]]. The case is the
[[claim-tomb-i-knee-lameness|knee-ankylosis]] of the Tomb I male, matched to the
historically documented spear wound that left Philip lame in 339 BC.

If accepted, this reassigns [[claim-tomb-ii-philip-ii]] to Arrhidaios (agreeing
with [[claim-tomb-ii-arrhidaios]] on Tomb II while disagreeing about where
Philip II lies). It is the minority view: it depends on associating a specific
pathology with one historical wound, and on dating Tomb I's occupants to
Philip's family. Held at 0.4 — a serious, published hypothesis that has not won
consensus.
