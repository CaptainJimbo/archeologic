**Peer-reviewed article, PNAS, 2015.** Fetched and verified via PubMed Central
(PMC4538655); full citation and DOI confirmed from the article record.

Shifts [[scholar-antonis-bartsiokas]]'s argument to a different tomb. Reports a
knee ankylosis (fused at ~79°, pierced by a healed penetrating lesion) in the
[[site-tomb-i]] male — see [[claim-tomb-i-knee-lameness]] — and reads it as the
lameness Philip II suffered from a 339 BC lance wound. Concludes Philip II lies in
Tomb I, with Cleopatra and their newborn; and, consequently, that
[[site-tomb-ii]] holds Arrhidaios and Eurydice.

Supports [[claim-tomb-i-philip-ii]]; disputes [[claim-tomb-ii-philip-ii]]. A
striking, much-discussed paper that nonetheless remains a minority position —
covered in the synthesis [[source-vergina-review-2023]].
