Greek archaeologist who excavated the [[site-great-tumulus]] at
[[site-aigai-vergina]] in 1977–78 and announced the unlooted royal tombs to the
world. He identified [[site-tomb-ii]] as the burial of **Philip II** — the
founding claim, [[claim-tomb-ii-philip-ii]] — and staked his professional legacy
on it.

His identification is the historical root of the entire debate: every later
paper, whether it defends or attacks Philip II, is arguing with Andronikos.
Tracking him as a node makes the graph show *why* the literature clusters the way
it does — the dispute is, in part, about whether the excavator got it right.
