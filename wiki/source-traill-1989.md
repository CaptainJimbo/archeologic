**Peer-reviewed article** by [[scholar-david-traill]], the chief modern sceptic of [[scholar-heinrich-schliemann]]. Fetched via Cambridge Core; the published *Extract* and full reference apparatus are real text (the body itself is access-gated, but the extract and citations are grounded here).

The piece dismantles the romantic image of the Schliemanns' fieldwork and, through Schliemann's diaries and letters in the Gennadius Library, exemplifies Traill's programme of showing that Schliemann's discovery narratives were embellished or fabricated — the same method behind his re-examination of 'Priam's Treasure' (JHS 104, 1984) and the co-edited *Myth, Scandal and History: The Heinrich Schliemann Controversy* (Calder & Traill 1986), both cited in its references.

This is a **primary node for the sceptic side** of [[claim-mask-authenticity-dispute]]: it establishes the documented unreliability that makes tampering/misreporting of the shaft-grave gold a live hypothesis. It does not itself prove the mask was altered — hence the sub-issue stays contested, not confirmed.
