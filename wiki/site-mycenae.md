Late Bronze Age citadel in the Argolid, the type-site of **Mycenaean** civilisation. **Grave Circle A**, a ring of six deep **shaft graves**, was excavated by [[scholar-heinrich-schliemann]] in 1876. The graves held five gold funerary face-masks; the most famous, from **Shaft Grave V**, is the so-called *Mask of Agamemnon* ([[claim-mask-of-agamemnon]]).

The grave-goods date the circle to **Late Helladic I (c. 1600–1500 BCE)** — the *beginning* of the Mycenaean sequence, roughly three centuries before the traditional dramatic date of the Trojan War (see [[claim-mask-chronological-gap]]). Schliemann discovered the citadel's Bronze Age reality, though the site had never been truly lost (see [[source-bha-insearch-2016]]). The absolute framework dating the shaft graves rests on radiocarbon-anchored Aegean chronology, not on Homer ([[source-manning-weninger-1992]]).
