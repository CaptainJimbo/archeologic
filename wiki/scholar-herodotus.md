Greek historian, 5th century BCE. In Histories 1.94 he reports that the Tyrrhenians (Etruscans) were Lydians who, driven by famine, migrated by sea from Anatolia to Umbria in Italy — the origin of the Eastern-migration hypothesis under test in [[claim-etruscan-anatolian-origin]]. Herodotus is treated here as the SOURCE OF THE CLAIM, an ancient literary report, not as physical evidence; his account was disputed in antiquity by [[scholar-dionysius-of-halicarnassus]] and is refuted as a recent demic migration by modern aDNA ([[source-posth-2021]], [[source-ghirotto-2013]]).
