**Peer-reviewed article, Science, 2006.** Fetched and verified via the PubMed
record (PMID 16645088); author list and result confirmed.

Reports an olive tree buried alive in growth position by the Thera tephra.
Radiocarbon wiggle-matching of successive tree-ring segments constrains the
eruption to **1627–1600 BCE at 95.4% probability** — about a century earlier than
the Egyptian-synchronism date. The keystone exhibit for
[[claim-thera-high-chronology]].

A root of the high-chronology citation funnel: much later argument leans on this
single buried branch, which is also why the branch itself became contested (its
ring count and the assumption it died in the eruption have both been questioned).
A clean example of why the graph distinguishes a primary datum from the many
papers that echo it.
