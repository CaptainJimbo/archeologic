Wooded hilltop near Nebra in the Ziegelroda Forest, Saxony-Anhalt, central Germany, given as the findspot of the Nebra Sky Disc and its accompanying bronze hoard. The material was not recovered in a controlled excavation: it was dug up by unlicensed metal detectorists in 1999 and passed through the black market before police recovery in 2002. Only afterwards did archaeologists (Landesamt fuer Denkmalpflege Sachsen-Anhalt / State Museum of Prehistory, Halle) excavate the reported spot in detail to reconstruct the context.

The findspot is itself a contested node in the dispute. The Early Bronze Age case (see [[source-pernicka-2020]]) holds that detailed excavation plus sediment / corrosion-attachment analyses corroborated the looters testimony that the disc was buried here with the hoard around 1600 BC. The Iron-Age challenge ([[claim-nebra-iron-age-challenge]]) argues the disc may not even have come from the Mittelberg and may not belong with the hoard. Because provenance was reconstructed rather than observed, the site anchors both [[claim-nebra-disc-bronze-age]] and its challenge.
