Peer-reviewed forum response (AJA, open access BY-NC); full text fetched. [[scholar-frank-kolb|Kolb]] defends his argument that Late Bronze Age Troy was NOT a significant trade city and that the Korfmann excavation 'mingles actual findings with mere assumptions and wishful thinking' — noting the scarcity of genuine imported Mycenaean pottery and the absence of Black Sea trade goods. Undercuts the material basis for a historically important Troy, and thus a key support for the war's plausibility.
