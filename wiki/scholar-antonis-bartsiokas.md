Greek palaeoanthropologist (Democritus University of Thrace) and the most
persistent challenger of Andronikos's identification. Author of
[[source-bartsiokas-2000]] (Tomb II bones are Arrhidaios, cremated dry) and lead
author of [[source-bartsiokas-2015]] (Philip II is actually in
[[site-tomb-i]]).

His two papers anchor the two dissenting claims —
[[claim-tomb-ii-arrhidaios]] and [[claim-tomb-i-philip-ii]] — and between them
they force the field to treat the identification as an open forensic question
rather than settled fact.
