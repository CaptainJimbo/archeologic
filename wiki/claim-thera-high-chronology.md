The **radiocarbon-driven** date for the Bronze Age eruption that buried
[[site-akrotiri-thera]]: roughly the late 17th to mid-16th century BCE. The
foundational exhibit is the [[source-friedrich-2006|buried olive branch]]
wiggle-matched to 1627–1600 BCE; later annual-resolution ¹⁴C work
([[source-pearson-2018]]) supports a 16th-century date and narrows the offset.

**Contested against** [[claim-thera-low-chronology]], the archaeologically-derived
date about a century later. The gap matters: it re-times the entire east
Mediterranean Late Bronze Age and the Aegean–Egyptian synchronisms. Held at 0.6 —
the scientific dating has strengthened the high chronology, but the conflict with
Egyptian historical chronology is real and unresolved, so this is not called
settled.
