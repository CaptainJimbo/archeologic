Classicist and curator (Manchester Museum) who, with Richard Neave, created the
1980s facial reconstruction of the Tomb II skull and read its asymmetry as
evidence of Philip II's eye wound. Co-author of [[source-musgrave-2010]].

His reconstruction is the positive case that [[source-bartsiokas-2000]] set out
to dismantle — the orbital "scar" Prag read as a healed wound, Bartsiokas read as
a cremation artefact. Prag is therefore a hub where the [[claim-tomb-ii-philip-ii]]
argument and its rebuttal meet.
