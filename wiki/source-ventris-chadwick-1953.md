**The primary decipherment paper** — the citogenesis root of the whole Mycenaean-Greek field. Fetched via the Cambridge Core landing page for JHS vol. 73 (real published Extract and reference apparatus, not a login stub).

The grounded Extract states the setting directly: with the fuller publication of the Pylos material found by Blegen and the Knossos tablets found by Evans, it had at last become possible to undertake a systematic study of the texts in Linear Script B, and their decipherment was the central problem in Aegean archaeology. The Extract also records the authors overturning the prior view: Evans believed Linear B expressed a non-Greek Aegean language, whereas on their phonetic and morphological criteria our Mycenaean dialect appears to ally itself with Greek. A footnote explicitly rejects an alternative (van Windekens's Pelasgian) as a highly dubious language against which Greek fits the evidence.
