Homer's Troy/Ilios is identified with the settlement mound of [[site-troy-hisarlik|Hisarlik]] in the Troad. The identification is old (classical Greeks and Romans built Ilion on the spot) and was reinforced by [[scholar-heinrich-schliemann|Schliemann]]'s 1870s digs and all later excavation. This *site* identification is well established and largely uncontested — a separate, easier question from whether LBA Troy was important or whether a war occurred.
