**The main published objection** — the most substantial contemporary attempt to reject the decipherment, by A. J. Beattie of Edinburgh. Fetched via the Cambridge Core landing page for JHS vol. 76 (real published Extract, not a stub).

The grounded Extract fairly summarises Ventris's three claims before attacking them: (1) the language of all the Linear B writings is Greek, of a pre-Dorian kind allied to classical Arcadian and Cypriot; (2) the script is mainly a syllabary akin to the classical Cypriot syllabary; (3) the phonetic values can be recovered by studying sign frequency, position and combination together with ideograms. Beattie held the derivation of those values to be circular and arbitrary.

This node **disputes** [[claim-linear-b-greek]] and documents the initial skepticism the claim faced. It is included precisely to show the contest was had and lost: Beattie's critique did not persuade the field, and the reading of tablets discovered after 1953 (e.g. the Pylos tripod tablet) answered exactly the charge of circularity. Retained as a secondary source recording dissent, not as evidence against the consensus. Full text behind access control; grounded content is the publisher Extract.
