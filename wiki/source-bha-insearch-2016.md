**Open-access review** (Ubiquity Press; full text fetched cleanly) of Moore, Rowlands & Karadimas, *In Search of Agamemnon: Early Travellers to Mycenae* (2014). Context / provenance source for [[site-mycenae]] and [[scholar-heinrich-schliemann]].

Key grounded point: it stresses that 'while Schliemann discovered the Mycenaean world through his excavations, **he most certainly did not discover Mycenae itself**' — the site was visited and described by antiquaries and Grand Tourists from classical times onward, and the book 'charts the development of approaches to **dating the site**, particularly in the 19th century.' This tempers the Schliemann-as-lone-genius myth that underwrites the romantic Agamemnon label ([[claim-mask-of-agamemnon]]).

Used as a **secondary/echo** context source, not primary evidence for any dating or authenticity claim.
