German archaeologist who directed the Troy excavations from 1988. He argued that Late Bronze Age Troy (Troia VI) was a substantial fortified trading city commanding the Dardanelles — a reading that revived the plausibility of a historically significant Troy behind the epic. His interpretation was sharply contested by [[scholar-frank-kolb]].
