Radiocarbon geoscientist at the University of Arizona (long-time director of the NSF-Arizona AMS Laboratory and editor of the journal Radiocarbon). Jull is a rare figure who sits on both sides of the Shroud sampling debate with primary access to the material.

He was a co-author of the original 1988 dating team [[source-damon-1989-nature]] (Arizona was one of the three AMS labs). Decades later he co-authored [[source-freer-waters-jull-2026]], the direct microscopic examination of leftover Arizona sample fragments that found them consistent with the main Shroud cloth and free of the spliced repair material posited by the reweave hypothesis. His work is central to assessing [[claim-shroud-turin-first-century]] and the objection [[claim-shroud-repair-sample-objection]].
