German businessman-turned-excavator of Troy (Hisarlik), [[site-mycenae]], Tiryns and Orchomenos. On uncovering a richly-furnished body in Shaft Grave V at Mycenae in 1876 he reportedly cabled that he had 'gazed upon the face of Agamemnon' — the origin of the attribution challenged in [[claim-mask-of-agamemnon]]. He published the finds in *Mycenae* (1878) ([[source-schliemann-mycenae-1878]]).

Schliemann is credited with revealing the historical reality of Mycenaean civilisation, but his reporting is unreliable: a revisionist literature led by [[scholar-david-traill]] has documented fabricated and embellished excavation narratives (e.g. Priam's Treasure), feeding the authenticity questions of [[claim-mask-authenticity-dispute]]. His conclusions, including the Agamemnon identification, are today regarded as ill-founded.
