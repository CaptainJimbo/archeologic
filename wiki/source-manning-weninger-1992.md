**Peer-reviewed research article** by [[scholar-sturt-manning]] and B. Weninger, fetched via Cambridge Core (published *Extract* + full references are real text). Uses probabilistic archaeological wiggle-matching of radiocarbon dates from key Aegean sites and concludes that the **conventional Aegean Late Bronze Age chronology holds on independent radiocarbon evidence**, 'whatever the flaws in its original construction.'

Grounding role: it **supports [[claim-mask-chronological-gap]]** by showing the Aegean absolute-dating framework — within which Grave Circle A at [[site-mycenae]] sits in Late Helladic I, c. 1600 BCE — rests on scientific dating, **not on Homeric assumptions**. That is the epistemic point that makes the ~300-year gap to Agamemnon robust rather than circular.

**Honest scope note:** this paper concerns the *close* of the LBA (LH IIIB/C, ~1200 BCE), not the shaft graves directly; it is cited for the *reliability of the radiocarbon-anchored chronology as a whole*, and the specific LH I date of the graves is standard handbook chronology rather than a finding of this paper.
