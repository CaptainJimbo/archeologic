The chronological keystone that dismantles the direct-destruction thesis ([[claim-thera-destroyed-minoans]]).

**Two different ceramic phases.** The eruption fell during **Late Minoan IA**: the youngest pottery sealed beneath the Santorini ash and in the derived tsunami deposits on Crete is LM IA ([[source-bruins-2009]]). The wave of destructions that ended the Cretan palatial (Neopalatial) system belongs to **Late Minoan IB** — a demonstrably later phase, whose diagnostic Marine Style pottery post-dates the eruption.

**Size of the gap.** By the conventional archaeological chronology the offset is ~30-50 years (stated explicitly in [[source-pichler-schiering-1977]]). By the radiocarbon [[claim-thera-high-chronology|high chronology (~1627-1600 BCE)]] the eruption is pushed earlier still, widening the gap to ~100-150 years before the LM IB horizon (~1500-1450 BCE). Either way the eruption and the palace destructions are separated by at least a full ceramic phase.

**No second eruption to blame.** [[source-pichler-schiering-1977]] showed geologically that there was a single eruption, not two events ~50 years apart, so there is no volcanic paroxysm coinciding with the LM IB destructions. Their causes (internal upheaval, possible Mycenaean takeover, further earthquakes) are debated but are NOT the Theran eruption.

Held at 0.82: the LM IA / LM IB separation is broadly accepted; residual uncertainty is over the exact magnitude of the gap, which rides on the unresolved high-vs-low chronology dispute.
