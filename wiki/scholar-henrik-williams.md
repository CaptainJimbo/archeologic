Runologist and professor of Scandinavian languages at Uppsala University, one of the leading modern authorities on runic inscriptions. Williams upholds the mainstream verdict that the [[claim-kensington-runestone-genuine|Kensington Runestone]] is a modern forgery, pointing to runic and numeral forms (dotted runes, pentadic numerals, umlaut dots) and orthography that belong to 19th-century Scandinavia rather than the medieval period. His survey The Kensington Runestone: Fact and Fiction (Swedish-American Historical Quarterly, 2012) is the standard modern treatment; it was paywalled (Project MUSE) during this investigation and so is cited here as a lead rather than a fetched source. He is the contemporary runological voice confirming that the forgery consensus has only hardened since Flom.
