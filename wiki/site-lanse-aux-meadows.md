Norse settlement site on the northern tip of the Great Northern Peninsula of Newfoundland, Canada — the **only unambiguously confirmed Norse (Viking) site in the Americas** and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Discovered in 1960 by Helge Ingstad and excavated over the following years by his wife, archaeologist Anne Stine Ingstad ([[scholar-helge-anne-ingstad]]), with later campaigns by Parks Canada (Birgitta Wallace). The remains include several turf-walled halls and workshops of Icelandic/Greenlandic Norse type, plus diagnostically Norse finds: a bronze ring-headed cloak pin, a soapstone spindle whorl, iron boat nails, and evidence of iron smelting and woodworking. Interpreted as a short-lived base camp used for onward exploration further south, consistent with the sagas' 'Vinland.'

The site is the physical anchor of [[claim-norse-precolumbian-america]]. Its wood was dated to AD 1021 by [[source-kuitems-2021]] and to a calibrated AD 975-1020 range by [[source-nydal-1989]].
