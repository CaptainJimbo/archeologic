Peer-reviewed Nature letter; the landing page with the full abstract was fetched this run via the DOI. The paper describes surface imaging plus high-resolution X-ray tomography of the surviving fragments from [[site-antikythera-wreck]], enabling reconstruction of the gear function and roughly doubling the number of deciphered inscriptions. Key findings grounding [[claim-antikythera-astronomical-computer]]: the device is a unique Greek geared instrument built around the end of the 2nd century BCE that calculated and displayed celestial information (lunar phases, a luni-solar calendar); it predicted lunar and solar eclipses using Babylonian arithmetic-progression cycles; and its gearing mechanically realizes Hipparchos' theory of the Moon's variable motion, an unexpected degree of technical sophistication for the period. Primary decoding based on physical imaging of the artefact, not an echo. Authored by [[scholar-tony-freeth]] and the Antikythera Mechanism Research Project.
