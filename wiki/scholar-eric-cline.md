**Who.** Eric H. Cline is an American archaeologist and ancient historian (George Washington University), a specialist in the eastern Mediterranean Bronze Age and field archaeology in the Levant and Aegean.

**Relevance here.** Cline is the most visible modern proponent of the multi-causal, systems-collapse reading of the end of the Late Bronze Age, through his popular-but-scholarly book *1177 BC: The Year Civilization Collapsed* (Princeton, 2014). He argues that no single factor — and specifically not the Sea Peoples alone — brought down the interconnected palatial civilizations; rather a perfect storm of drought, famine, earthquakes (the so-called earthquake storms), disrupted international trade, internal rebellion and migration caused a fragile, globalized system to unravel. He treats the Sea Peoples as one component and partial symptom of that unravelling rather than its cause.
