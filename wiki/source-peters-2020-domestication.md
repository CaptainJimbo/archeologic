Review / reference synthesis by the excavation and archaeozoology team. Fetched this run as HTML from Springer Nature Link via the DOI; only the open preview was accessible (the State of Knowledge and Current Debates opening section plus the full reference list), so the note is grounded on that preview, not the paywalled body.

The entry is explicitly titled Agriculture and Domestication and is framed as State of Knowledge and Current Debates, which establishes that the agricultural and domestication status of the [[site-gobekli-tepe]] community is treated as a live scholarly question by the excavators themselves. The preview confirms the site setting (Germus range, ca. 770 m asl over the Harran plain, a 15 m mound ca. 300 m across), that the megalithic architecture was unrecognised until Klaus Schmidt's 1994 discovery, that excavation began in 1995, and that the site is best known for monumental round-oval buildings 10-30 m across.

Role for this investigation: it supports [[claim-gobekli-tepe-pre-agricultural]] while carrying its central nuance, namely the ongoing debate over whether the builders were pure foragers or very early cultivators. Authored by [[scholar-klaus-schmidt]], [[scholar-oliver-dietrich]] and colleagues. Secondary/review status: a survey of the evidence rather than a primary excavation datum, and only the preview was verifiable this run.
