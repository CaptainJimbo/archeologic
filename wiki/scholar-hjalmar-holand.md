Norwegian-American writer and amateur historian (1872-1963) who acquired the [[claim-kensington-runestone-genuine|Kensington Runestone]] from Olof Ohman around 1907 and became its lifelong champion, producing a long series of books defending its authenticity and tying it to a supposed 14th-century Norse expedition (the Paul Knutson expedition). Holand had no training in runology or historical linguistics, and professional philologists — from [[scholar-george-flom]] and Rasmus B. Anderson ([[source-anderson-1920]]) onward — rejected his arguments. He is the principal figure of the authenticity-defending minority; the node is retained, with a supports edge to the refuted claim, to represent that minority honestly, not because the position carries scholarly weight.
