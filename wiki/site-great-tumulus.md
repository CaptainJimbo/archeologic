The great earthen mound at [[site-aigai-vergina]] raised over the royal tombs,
excavated by [[scholar-manolis-andronikos]] in 1977–78. It covers at least three
tombs: the looted [[site-tomb-i]], the unlooted [[site-tomb-ii]], and Tomb III
(the "Prince's Tomb").

The tumulus is the container that ties the whole Vergina dispute together — the
argument is really about *which body in which chamber under this mound* is which
Macedonian royal. It is the natural hub node of the cluster.
