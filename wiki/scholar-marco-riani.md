Statistician at the University of Parma, specialist in robust regression and the 'forward search' method (developed with Anthony C. Atkinson). With Atkinson and collaborators he applied these tools to the twelve raw subsample results of the 1988 Shroud dating.

He is lead author of [[source-riani-2013-heterogeneity]], which reports statistically significant heterogeneity and a spatial trend in the 1988 results, and of a later Entropy (2020) proactive-analysis follow-up. This body of work is the most methodologically credible support for [[claim-shroud-repair-sample-objection]] — the argument that the single sampled corner may not represent the whole cloth. Riani argues about sampling adequacy and does not himself claim a 1st-century date; his co-author Giulio Fanti, by contrast, is an outspoken authenticity advocate whose interpretations should be weighed for bias.
