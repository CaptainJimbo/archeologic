Old Testament scholar (University of Stellenbosch, South Africa). Author of the two fetched open-access studies used in this investigation, [[source-bosman-2002]] on the impact of archaeology on the interpretation of Exodus and [[source-bosman-2014]] on the exodus as cultural memory and founding myth.

His position is methodological rather than partisan: he surveys the archaeological difficulties (notably the absence of any trace of Israel as a people in Egypt in the Late Bronze or Early Iron Age) and argues the theological meaning of the exodus does not stand or fall on its literal historicity.
