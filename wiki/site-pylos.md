Late Bronze Age Mycenaean palace in southwest Messenia, excavated by Carl Blegen from 1939. It yielded the largest and best-preserved [[claim-linear-b-greek|Linear B]] archive on the Greek mainland — the tablets whose fuller publication (The Pylos Tablets, ed. E. L. Bennett, 1951) made the systematic decipherment possible.

Blegen's 1952 season produced the tripod tablet PY Ta 641, whose Greek reading ti-ri-po-de (tripods) with matching ideograms gave the decipherment its most celebrated independent confirmation, since those tablets were found after Ventris's grid values were fixed. A place node, not a document; distinct from [[site-mycenae]] and [[site-minoan-crete|Knossos]], the other two find-spots named in [[source-ventris-chadwick-1953]].
