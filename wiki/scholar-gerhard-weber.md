**Gerhard W. Weber** is an evolutionary anthropologist at the University of Vienna (Department of Evolutionary Anthropology; Human Evolution and Archaeological Science) and lead author of the 2025 Scientific Reports study [[source-weber-2025-octagon]] that re-examined the Octagon cranium from Ephesos using morphology, ancient DNA and radiocarbon dating. That work established the remains are those of an adolescent boy of Italian/Sardinian genetic ancestry, not Cleopatra VII's half-sister Arsinoe IV, thereby dismantling a widely cited physical argument for African ancestry in the Ptolemaic family.
