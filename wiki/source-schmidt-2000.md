Primary excavation report. Fetched this run as open-access HTML from the Persee landing page (persee.fr) via the DOI; the article page and its keyword set and introduction grounded this note.

This is the excavator's first published account of the 1995-1999 excavations at [[site-gobekli-tepe]]. The keyword set (Upper Mesopotamia, Fertile Crescent, Golden Triangle, PPN, Neolithization, Megaliths, Pillars, Religion) situates the site in the Pre-Pottery Neolithic and frames its megalithic pillars as evidence for early cult. The introduction traces the site from Peter Benedict's 1960s Istanbul-Chicago survey (recorded as V52/1, a flint-littered limestone ridge) to Schmidt's own recognition of buried monumental architecture.

As the foundational primary report by [[scholar-klaus-schmidt]], it anchors the PPN dating and monumental character that underpin [[claim-gobekli-tepe-pre-agricultural]]. Note: the Persee record mis-lists the author initial as Katja; the excavator and author is Klaus Schmidt. Primary field evidence, not an echo.
