**Peer-reviewed article, Radiocarbon, 2009 (open access).** Fetched via Cambridge Core (DOI 10.1017/S003382220005579X); the full abstract was returned and grounds this note.

Primary geoarchaeology. The team found extensive **tsunami deposits at Palaikastro on the north coast of Crete** containing reworked volcanic Santorini ash — physical proof the eruption reached Crete (airborne ashfall preceded a tsunami generated during a later eruption phase). So the eruption's effects on Crete are REAL, which is why the popular claim is not simply nonsense.

But two data points cut against direct destruction: (1) the **youngest pottery in the tsunami deposits is Late Minoan IA**, fixing the eruption in LM IA — before the LM IB palace destructions ([[claim-thera-lmib-gap]]); (2) the radiocarbon dates on animal bone in the deposits (~3350 BP) match the Akrotiri eruption date, wiggle-matched to **1627-1600 cal BCE** ([[claim-thera-high-chronology]]), ~100-150 yr older than the Egyptian-synchronism date the authors note is 'conventionally linked at ~1500 BCE.'

Supports [[claim-thera-lmib-gap]] and disputes [[claim-thera-destroyed-minoans]]: the eruption struck Crete during LM IA, not at the LM IB destruction horizon. A primary datum (excavated tsunami stratigraphy + on-site 14C), not an echo.
