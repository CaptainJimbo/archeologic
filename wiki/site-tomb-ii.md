The unlooted, two-chambered barrel-vaulted tomb under the [[site-great-tumulus]],
found intact in 1977 with a wealth of royal armour, gold, and two cremation
burials (a man in the main chamber, a woman in the antechamber).

Its main-chamber male is the single most fought-over skeleton in Greek
archaeology: claimed for Philip II ([[claim-tomb-ii-philip-ii]]) and for Philip
III Arrhidaios ([[claim-tomb-ii-arrhidaios]]). Because the tomb bears no
inscription naming its occupant, the identification rests entirely on
[[claim-tomb-ii-green-cremation|forensic]] and chronological argument — which is
exactly why it stays contested.
