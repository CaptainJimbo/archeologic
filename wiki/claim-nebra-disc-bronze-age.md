The Nebra Sky Disc — a ~30 cm bronze disc with gold inlays showing a sun or full moon, a crescent, stars including what is read as the Pleiades, and later horizon arcs and a solar barque — is dated by most scholars to the central European Early Bronze Age Unetice culture, around 1600 BCE, and is celebrated as one of the oldest concrete depictions of the cosmos (UNESCO Memory of the World).

WHY THE DATE IS INDIRECT: the object was looted, not excavated. It was dug up by metal detectorists on the [[site-nebra-mittelberg]] in 1999 and recovered by police in 2002; there is no scientific method to directly date the copper alloy. As even its defenders state ([[source-pernicka-2020]]), the ~1600 BCE date rests on reconstructing the find context and radiocarbon/typological dating of the accompanying hoard (swords, axes, bracelets, chisel), plus material analyses linking disc, hoard and site.

SUPPORTING MATERIAL EVIDENCE (fetched this run): gold-provenance geochemistry tying the first-phase inlays to Cornish sources within an EBA metal-trade network [[source-ehser-2011]]; metallographic study showing manufacture by flat casting then repeated hot forging, consistent with EBA sheet-bronze technology [[source-dieck-2024]]; and the interdisciplinary corrosion/sediment, gold and lead-isotope synthesis of [[source-pernicka-2020]] corroborating burial with the hoard.

LIVE MINORITY CHALLENGE: in 2020 Gebhard and Krause ([[scholar-gebhard-krause]]) argued the disc could be Iron Age and might not derive from the Mittelberg or from the hoard at all — see [[claim-nebra-iron-age-challenge]]. This was strongly rebutted by [[scholar-ernst-pernicka]] and colleagues, and the EBA dating remains consensus.

VERDICT: SUPPORTED but not settled. The genuineness (not a modern forgery) is very well established; the EBA date is the strong majority view resting on a coherent, multiply-analysed material and contextual case. Confidence held at 0.65 rather than higher because the anchoring context is reconstructed from a looted find and a credentialed minority has published a still-unretracted alternative dating — a real, live scholarly dispute rather than a fringe claim.
