British mathematician and filmmaker, a leading figure of the Antikythera Mechanism Research Project and honorary professor at UCL. Freeth led the 2005 application of Microfocus X-ray Computed Tomography and Polynomial Texture Mapping to the 82 fragments, which decoded the rear dials and roughly doubled the number of legible inscriptions. He is lead author of the two most cited modern decodings: the 2006 Nature letter [[source-freeth-2006-nature]] establishing eclipse prediction via Babylonian arithmetic cycles and a mechanization of Hipparchos' lunar theory, the 2014 PLoS ONE eclipse-scheme paper [[source-freeth-2014-eclipse]], and the 2021 Scientific Reports reconstruction of the front-face Cosmos display [[source-freeth-2021-cosmos]]. His work is the empirical backbone of [[claim-antikythera-astronomical-computer]].
