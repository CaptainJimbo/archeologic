**Peer-reviewed article, Radiocarbon, 1989.** Abstract and reference list fetched this run via Cambridge Core (DOI 10.1017/S0033822200012613); the returned abstract grounds this note.

Primary radiocarbon lab work, decades before Kuitems. Nydal (Radiological Dating Laboratory, Norwegian Institute of Technology) re-evaluated the C-14 series from [[site-lanse-aux-meadows]] — a site he records as discovered by Helge Ingstad in 1960. The chief earlier problem was that some dated material was driftwood (old-wood offset); Nydal concludes the site charcoal shows no greater error than normal settlement sites, and with an assumed systematic error of 30 +/- 20 years derives a calibrated range of **AD 975-1020**, agreeing with the historically expected ~AD 1000 and corroborated by high-precision accelerator dating at Toronto.

A second, independent primary line of evidence supporting [[claim-norse-precolumbian-america]]. Its AD 975-1020 window brackets the later, single-year 1021 result of [[source-kuitems-2021]] — an example of convergent dating rather than citogenesis. It cites the excavation monographs of Anne Stine and Helge Ingstad ([[scholar-helge-anne-ingstad]]).
