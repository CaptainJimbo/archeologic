Classicist (University of California, Davis), the leading modern critic of [[scholar-heinrich-schliemann]]'s reliability. With William M. Calder III he edited *Myth, Scandal and History: The Heinrich Schliemann Controversy* (1986) and wrote *Schliemann of Troy: Treasure and Deceit* (1995) and the collected *Excavating Schliemann* (1993). His re-examinations of Schliemann's diaries and letters argue that key discovery narratives were embellished or fabricated (see [[source-traill-1989]], reviewed in [[source-easton-1994]]).

Traill and Calder are central to [[claim-mask-authenticity-dispute]] — the argument that some shaft-grave gold, possibly including the *Mask of Agamemnon*, may have been 'improved' by a modern goldsmith or otherwise misreported. Critics (including Easton) hold that Traill sometimes overstates the case, which is why the sub-issue remains genuinely contested rather than settled.
