Peer-reviewed geochemical provenance study; abstract fetched this run via the DOI landing page. Primary lab evidence bearing on the material identity of the disc.

Using LA-ICP-MS, the authors compared the composition of a fragment of the disc gold sun-sheet (the first of three gold-application phases, comprising the sun or full-moon disc, crescent and 32 stars) against 66 native gold particles from Cornish placer and lode deposits. The Carnon placers at Devoran and Feock, when mixed, correlate in trace elements (Co, Ni, Pd, Ag, Sn, Sb) with the sun-sheet gold; earlier comparison with central and southeastern European gold showed no similarity. Lead-isotope comparison of the hoard bronze with 18 Cornish copper ores showed no correlation, excluding Cornwall as the copper source. The paper concludes the first-phase gold likely originates from Cornwall, implying substantial Early Bronze Age metal trade from the British Isles to central Germany.

Relevance to the dating dispute: the disc is analysed as an Early Bronze Age object embedded in an EBA long-distance metal-trade network; the geochemical fingerprinting is part of the material-science case that grounds [[claim-nebra-disc-bronze-age]].
