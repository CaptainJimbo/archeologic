Greek archaeologist (1936 to 2010), excavator with his wife Efi Sapouna-Sakellaraki of Archanes and the Anemospilia shrine ([[site-anemospilia]]) on Crete. A curator at the National Archaeological Museum in Athens and a leading figure in Minoan field archaeology.

He is best known here for the 1979 excavation at Anemospilia and for the interpretation, published in the Archanes monograph and popularized in a 1981 National Geographic feature, that the collapsed building preserved a human sacrifice interrupted by an earthquake ([[claim-anemospilia-sacrifice]]). The reading remains contested, but the excavation itself is meticulously documented and is the single strongest datum behind the broader claim that [[claim-minoan-human-sacrifice|the Minoans practiced human sacrifice]].

The skulls of the Anemospilia dead were later cast and facially reconstructed by a Manchester team ([[source-musgrave-1994]]).
