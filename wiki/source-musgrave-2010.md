**Peer-reviewed article, International Journal of Medical Sciences, 2010
(open access).** Verified via the journal record and Bristol's research
repository.

A point-by-point rebuttal of [[source-bartsiokas-2000]]. Its central forensic
claim: the colour and transverse fracture lines of the Tomb II bones indicate a
**green** (fleshed) cremation, not a dry one — see
[[claim-tomb-ii-green-cremation]]. The authors argue this weakens rather than
strengthens the Arrhidaios case, and that the skull's facial asymmetry is
consistent with Philip II's eye wound. Supports [[claim-tomb-ii-philip-ii]];
disputes [[claim-tomb-ii-arrhidaios]].

Authored by the [[scholar-john-prag|Prag]]–Neave–[[scholar-jonathan-musgrave|Musgrave]]
team behind the original facial reconstruction, so it is **evidence *and*
interested party** — a good example of why the graph tracks who argues what, not
just the conclusion.
