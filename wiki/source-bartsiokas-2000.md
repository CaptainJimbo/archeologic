**Peer-reviewed article, Science, 2000.** Verified via search of the Science
record and the surrounding literature (the paper is widely cited by both camps).

Re-examines the cremated Tomb II skull. Argues that the orbital scar earlier read
as [[scholar-john-prag|Prag]]'s evidence of Philip II's famous eye wound is in
fact an artefact of cremation, and that the post-cranial skeleton lacks the
trauma Philip's documented injuries would predict. Concludes the bones were burned
**dry** (degreased, unfleshed) and belong to [[scholar-antonis-bartsiokas]]'s
proposed occupant, Philip III Arrhidaios — i.e. it supports
[[claim-tomb-ii-arrhidaios]] and attacks [[claim-tomb-ii-philip-ii]].

This is a **primary node in the dispute**: directly answered by
[[source-musgrave-2010]]. In graph terms it is one root of the Tomb II
citation funnel — a large share of the "Arrhidaios" argument traces back here.
