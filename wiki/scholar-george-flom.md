American Germanic philologist and runologist (University of Illinois; later University of Iowa), a specialist in Old Norse and Scandinavian dialectology. In [[source-flom-1910]] he produced one of the earliest expert refutations of the [[claim-kensington-runestone-genuine|Kensington Runestone]], demonstrating that its language is modern Scandinavian rather than the Old Swedish of 1362 and that its runes are modern characters. He represents the professional-philological consensus that formed against the stone within a decade of its 1898 appearance.
