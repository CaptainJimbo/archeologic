The grave-goods of Grave Circle A at [[site-mycenae]] — pottery styles, the shaft-grave assemblage as a whole — place its burials in **Late Helladic I, c. 1600–1550 BCE**, at the very start of the Mycenaean sequence. The traditional date of the Trojan War, and therefore of any Agamemnon, is c. 1250 BCE (Late Helladic IIIB). The mask thus predates its supposed owner by roughly **three centuries**.

**Status: supported (high confidence).** This is uncontested textbook chronology in Aegean prehistory, and the absolute framework does not depend on Homer: independent **radiocarbon** dating anchors the Aegean Late Bronze Age sequence (see [[source-manning-weninger-1992]]). Because the dating is secure and the gap is large, this claim **disputes** and effectively **refutes** [[claim-mask-of-agamemnon]]. The dating stands regardless of the separate authenticity question ([[claim-mask-authenticity-dispute]]): even if genuine, the object is far too early to be Agamemnon's.
