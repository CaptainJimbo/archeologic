Israeli archaeologist (Tel Aviv University), a leading figure in the archaeology of early Israel and co-author with Neil Asher Silberman of The Bible Unearthed (2001). On the basis of highland settlement surveys he argues that early Israel emerged from within Canaanite society in Iron Age I rather than arriving from Egypt, and that the exodus and conquest narratives are largely a later literary construction reflecting the geopolitical horizon of the 7th century BCE Kingdom of Judah.

He is the principal voice disputing [[claim-exodus-historical]] as a large-scale event and is skeptical even of a recoverable historical kernel [[claim-exodus-small-kernel]], though he acknowledges the tradition preserves cultural memory. Paired here with [[scholar-william-dever]], who reaches similar conclusions about the large-scale claim while remaining somewhat more open to a small kernel.
