The ancient first capital of the kingdom of Macedon, at modern Vergina in northern
Greece — a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its identification as Aigai (rather than
Edessa, an older guess) is now settled, reinforced by the royal necropolis and
palace.

Aigai is the geographic anchor of the Vergina cluster. It contains the
[[site-great-tumulus]], which in turn contains [[site-tomb-i]] and
[[site-tomb-ii]]. As a *site* node it is a place, not a document — no source
citation is attached, by design: the anti-hallucination rule governs source
nodes, and places are established context.
