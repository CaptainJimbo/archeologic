**Primary excavation publication** — the root of the whole affair. Schliemann's own 1878 account of the [[site-mycenae]] and Tiryns digs, where the Grave Circle A finds (shaft graves, gold masks) were first published and where the Agamemnon identification originates ([[claim-mask-of-agamemnon]]).

Fetched via the Cambridge Core landing page for the 2010 reissue (real publisher text, not a login stub). The publisher's description explicitly names the finds — 'most famously the death mask of Agamemnon and the shaft graves' — and delivers the modern verdict in the same breath: 'while today his methods of excavation are deplored and **many of his conclusions thought to be ill-founded**, he is rightly credited with the discovery of the lost and ancient Mycenaean civilisation.'

So this node does double duty: it is the **primary source of the claim** (citogenesis root — every 'Mask of Agamemnon' caption traces back here) *and* documents that the attribution is discredited.
