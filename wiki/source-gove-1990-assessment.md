**Peer-reviewed assessment, Radiocarbon, 1990.** The Cambridge Core landing page was fetched this run via the DOI; the full abstract and reference list were extracted and grounded this note (full PDF is gated but the abstract carries the quantitative findings).

Harry Gove was a co-inventor of AMS radiocarbon dating and an outside observer of the Shroud project, which makes this an informed independent appraisal rather than a self-report by the dating teams. The abstract confirms the headline numbers of [[source-damon-1989-nature]]: 'a calibrated calendar age range of AD 1260-1390 for the linen of the Turin shroud at a 95% confidence level,' measured independently at Arizona, Oxford, and ETH Zurich with British Museum support. Gove concludes the results are 'credible' despite noting that procedures diverged from those recommended at the Pontifical Academy of Sciences workshop, and he catalogues 'many doubts... both real and fanciful' that had been raised.

This is a secondary source (an assessment of the primary measurement, not a new measurement), but a high-quality one from an authority with no stake in the outcome. It corroborates the medieval date and thus supports the refutation of [[claim-shroud-turin-first-century]]. Its candid airing of doubts is also the honest counterweight recorded under [[claim-shroud-repair-sample-objection]].
