**The claim.** Cleopatra VII (69-30 BC) belonged to the [[site-alexandria|Alexandria]]-based Ptolemaic dynasty, founded by Ptolemy I Soter, a Macedonian Greek general of Alexander the Great. By ancestry and culture the Ptolemies were overwhelmingly Macedonian Greek: they ruled in Greek, took Greek throne-names, and famously practised sibling and cousin marriage, which kept the royal line largely endogamous within a Macedonian Greek elite. Cleopatra herself was culturally Greek and was reputedly the first Ptolemy to bother learning the Egyptian language.

**Primary evidence.** [[source-plutarch-antony|Plutarch, Life of Antony 27]] records that the kings of Egypt before her had not even made an effort to learn the native (Egyptian) language, and that some had actually given up their Macedonian dialect. This near-contemporary Greek source both confirms that the dynasty was Macedonian-Greek-speaking and singles out Cleopatra as exceptional for her linguistic range, consistent with her being the first of the line to learn Egyptian.

**Scope and calibration.** What is well supported is the predominantly Greek/Macedonian reading: dynastic origin, language, court culture, and a heavily endogamous pedigree are all documented. What is NOT securely established is the stronger claim of wholly or purely Greek descent, because the identity of Cleopatra's mother and of one paternal grandmother is not documented (see [[claim-cleopatra-maternal-uncertain]]). Popular claims of substantial Egyptian or sub-Saharan African ancestry are speculative and unproven; the osteological evidence once invoked for them has collapsed (see [[source-weber-2025-octagon]]). Held at 0.78: strong for predominantly Greek, with a reserve for the undocumented maternal lines. Scholars such as [[scholar-duane-roller]] reach a similar predominantly-Greek-with-an-unknown-remainder position.
