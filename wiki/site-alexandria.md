**Alexandria (Alexandria ad Aegyptum)**, founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BC on the Egyptian Mediterranean coast, was the royal capital of the Ptolemaic kingdom and the seat of Cleopatra VII. It was a fundamentally Greek foundation and civic institution: a Greek-speaking court, the Mouseion and Library, and a citizen body organised on the Hellenic polis model, set within, but administratively distinct from, native Egypt. It is the setting for the Ptolemaic Greek court culture described in [[source-plutarch-antony]] and underlies the cultural dimension of [[claim-cleopatra-greek-ancestry]].
