Italian archaeologist (1874-1937) of the Italian Archaeological School, excavator of the Phaistos palace on [[site-minoan-crete]]. On 3 July 1908 he reported recovering the disk from a Middle Minoan context in the palace's north-east apartments, alongside a Linear A tablet and datable pottery.

Pernier sits at the centre of both claims. In the mainstream account his documented find-context is a load-bearing argument FOR authenticity ([[claim-phaistos-disc-authentic]]). In the forgery hypothesis ([[claim-phaistos-forgery-hypothesis]]) he is the alleged fabricator or commissioner — the charge being that a young excavator manufactured a spectacular find. Because the museum has never permitted thermoluminescence testing, neither reading can be closed by laboratory evidence, which is exactly why the dispute persists.
