**What.** Ugarit was a wealthy cosmopolitan port kingdom on the northern Syrian coast (modern Ras Shamra), a hub of Late Bronze Age international trade and diplomacy and home to an important cuneiform archive including alphabetic Ugaritic texts. Its destruction and abandonment early in the 12th century BCE is one of the anchor events of the Late Bronze Age collapse.

**Why it matters to the claim.** Ugarit is the site most often cited for the Sea Peoples narrative: late letters from the archive (e.g. correspondence mentioning enemy ships and requests for help) are read as eyewitness testimony to sea-borne attack shortly before the city fell, and the city was not reoccupied. It is thus a key test case for [[claim-sea-peoples-collapse]]. But it is also the setting of the palaeoclimate evidence in [[source-kaniewski-2013]] (the nearby coastal site of Gibala-Tell Tweini lay within the kingdom of Ugarit), which places the destruction within a context of drought and famine — feeding the multi-causal reading [[claim-lbac-multicausal]]. The destruction was long attributed monocausally to the Sea Peoples, but its exact cause and date remain debated.
