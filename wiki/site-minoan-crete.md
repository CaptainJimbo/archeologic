The island of Crete and its Bronze Age Minoan palace centres — Knossos, Malia, Phaistos, Zakros — the 'civilization' whose alleged destruction by the Thera eruption is under examination ([[claim-thera-destroyed-minoans]]).

The relevant sequence is the Neopalatial period, spanning **Late Minoan IA** (during which the eruption occurred and buried [[site-akrotiri-thera]] ~120 km to the north) and **Late Minoan IB** (during which a wave of destructions ended most palace centres). The gap between those two phases is the whole argument ([[claim-thera-lmib-gap]]).

A place node, not a document. Distinct from [[site-akrotiri-thera]], which is the Cycladic town on Thera itself; this node is the Cretan mainland target of the destruction claim. Tsunami evidence has been documented on its north coast at Palaikastro and Malia (see [[source-bruins-2009]], [[source-lespez-2021]]).
