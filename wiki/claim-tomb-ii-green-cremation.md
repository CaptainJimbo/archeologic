The forensic crux of the whole Vergina debate. **Green cremation** = the body was
burned soon after death with flesh still on the bone; **dry cremation** = a
skeleton burned long after decomposition. The two leave different signatures in
bone colour and in the pattern of warping and fracture lines.

[[source-bartsiokas-2000]] argued for **dry** cremation, supporting
[[claim-tomb-ii-arrhidaios]] (a delayed, disturbed reburial).
[[source-musgrave-2010]] argued for **green** cremation — transverse fracture
patterns and colouration consistent with a fleshed body — which supports a fresh
royal pyre and therefore [[claim-tomb-ii-philip-ii]].

This note is the single point where the physical evidence most directly decides
between the two Tomb II identifications, which is why both camps invest so heavily
in it. Confidence 0.6: the green reading is somewhat better supported in the
recent literature, but the dry reading is not dead.
