**Peer-reviewed article, Scientific Reports, 2023 (open access).** Verified via
the Nature record and PMC (PMC10147620); the fetch hit an IdP redirect but
citation and result are confirmed from the indexed record and abstract.

Reports a second, independent buried olive — a shrub carbonised in life position
on neighbouring Therasia — radiocarbon-dated to the **mid-16th century BCE**,
slightly younger than the [[source-friedrich-2006|1627–1600 BCE branch]]. The
authors read it as support for a high (16th-c) date while noting the persistent
gap with the archaeological chronology.

A useful stress-test node: a *new primary datum* rather than a review, it nudges
[[claim-thera-high-chronology]] later within the high-chronology range and
tightens the case against [[claim-thera-low-chronology]] — showing the graph can
represent evidence that supports one claim while only partly agreeing with an
allied source.
