**Peer-reviewed Letter, Nature, 1977.** Fetched via the Nature record (DOI 10.1038/267819a0); the full abstract was returned and is the basis for this note (the body text is paywalled).

The abstract is directly load-bearing for the destruction debate. It states that '[[scholar-spyridon-marinatos|Marinatos]]' theory of the decline of the Minoan civilisation by paroxysmal volcanic events of the Thera volcano is widely accepted,' then identifies 'the main and controversial problem': the LM IB devastations of Cretan sites and palaces are dated archaeologically as **30-50 yr later** than the LM IA destruction of Akrotiri on Santorini. Marinatos' theory would therefore require TWO eruptions (~1500 BCE for LM IA, ~1450 BCE for LM IB).

The authors' own fieldwork on Santorini finds 'neither products of two volcanic outbursts nor of a two-phase eruption... differing in a time interval of about 50 yr' — i.e. a SINGLE eruption. Load-bearing consequence: with only one eruption (in LM IA), there is no volcanic event to blame for the later LM IB Cretan destructions. Cited for [[claim-thera-lmib-gap]] and against [[claim-thera-destroyed-minoans]].

A near-primary node in the citation graph: a geological field study, not a textbook echo, and one of the two founding 1977-78 Nature papers (with Sparks et al. 1978) that first pinned down the chronological separation.
