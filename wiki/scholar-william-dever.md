American archaeologist (University of Arizona), a prominent authority on Syro-Palestinian and biblical archaeology and author of works including Who Were the Early Israelites and Where Did They Come From? (2003). Dever holds that the archaeological record contains no evidence for a large-scale exodus or a unified military conquest, and that early Israel is best explained as an indigenous development among displaced Canaanites and pastoralists in the central highlands.

He disputes [[claim-exodus-historical]] as literally stated. Compared with [[scholar-israel-finkelstein]], Dever is generally read as somewhat more willing to entertain a small historical core behind the tradition, which is why he is cited on the contested side of [[claim-exodus-small-kernel]]. Both scholars are widely regarded as representing the mainstream archaeological consensus against the biblical-magnitude reading.
