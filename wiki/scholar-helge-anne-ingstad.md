Norwegian explorer Helge Ingstad (1899-2001) and his wife, archaeologist Anne Stine Ingstad (1918-1997). In 1960 Helge, guided by the Vinland sagas and local knowledge, identified turf-mound remains at [[site-lanse-aux-meadows]]; Anne Stine then led the scientific excavations (1961-1968) that established the site as Norse.

Their work provided the first incontrovertible archaeological proof of pre-Columbian European contact with the Americas, transforming [[claim-norse-precolumbian-america]] from saga-based conjecture into established fact. Anne Stine's multi-volume excavation monograph 'The Discovery of a Norse Settlement in America' (Norwegian Univ. Press, 1977) and Helge's companion volume are the primary field record, cited by later dating studies including [[source-nydal-1989]].
