The claim. The Phaistos Disk — a fired-clay disk about 16 cm across, stamped on both faces with a spiral of 241-242 undeciphered signs in 61 boxes, reported by Luigi Pernier (see [[scholar-luigi-pernier]]) at the Phaistos palace on [[site-minoan-crete]] in 1908 — is a genuine Bronze Age Minoan artifact, not a modern fabrication.

Mainstream standing (primary vs echo). This is the consensus. The specialists who have handled the object read it as authentic and merely undeciphered: Arthur Evans dated it in 1921 (see [[scholar-arthur-evans]]), Yves Duhoux to 1850-1600 BC in 1977, and Louis Godart (see [[scholar-louis-godart]]) to the Middle/Late Minoan range in 1995 — all recorded in the fetched [[source-reczko-2009]]. A 2021 reference entry ([[source-encyclopedic-dict-archaeology-2021]]) likewise places it, without caveat, in a deposit dated c 1700 BC, contemporary with Linear A. Both fetched sources are secondary/reference echoes of consensus rather than the original excavation report, but they document that authenticity is the settled baseline.

Why not certainty (held at 0.68). Two things keep this SUPPORTED rather than confirmed. First, the disk is genuinely anomalous — a unique stamped-clay document with no comparanda — which is what gives the forgery hypothesis ([[claim-phaistos-forgery-hypothesis]]) its opening. Second, and decisively, the object has never been laboratory-tested: the Heraklion museum has declined thermoluminescence dating, so authenticity rests on find-context, style and expert judgement, not on a physical date for the clay. Authenticity is well supported and very probably correct, but it is not lab-confirmed.

Verdict. SUPPORTED at 0.68. The overwhelming weight of specialist opinion, backed by a documented Middle Minoan find-context, holds the disk genuine; the forgery hypothesis is a real but minority position kept unresolvable only by the absence of a thermoluminescence test. A settled-enough claim with an honest, still-open forgery sub-debate, extending the Minoan cluster around [[site-minoan-crete]].
